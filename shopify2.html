Shopify

Product & Data Viz Designer, Winter 2017

At Shopify I redesigned the dashboard to enhance how merchants make decisions regarding their business. I got to explore the problem, unpack the people problem, test iterations and collaborated with engineering plan the development.

PROBLEM

Dashboards should be scannable and easy to understand. A common complaint with the current dashboard is that it is high in information density and there is no way to customize it. We wanted to create a dashboard that was easy to understand, and also include the extra information it needed.

INITIAL RESEARCH

In January 2017 Shopify saw an increase in support desk ticketes relating to the dashboard. I dug deeper into the problem by reading through the support desk tickets and doing some user interviews, mainly watching as users approached the dashboard section of Shopify.

We saw two major problems with the dashboard and how information is laid out:

  • Where information is placed doesn't make sense to merchants. They don't understand how to get from the dashboard to reports.
  • The data visualization types are dated, merchants are not able to gain useful information from the way the dashboard is currently laid out
  • Dashboard serve different functions for different types of businesses and as business maturity grows.

DATA MAPPING

In order to determine what metrics to surface on the dashboard, I collaborated with our data engineering team to sync up on what metrics were on their roadmap. I mapped out the data points that we could surface to our merchants, using two different schemas.

The first schema maps out information depending on business vertical. In this case, I bolded metrics that users mentioned were particularily valuable.

Pros: Tells a more accurate story of the data (merchants usually make business decisions depending on verticals), easy to share with specific members of the business, scales for more types of businesses

Cons: Some metrics will not be populated (small businesses might not have sales data until the first month)

For the second schema, I explored sectioning off data by business type. Shopify has 3 main business types that need vastly different metrics to make business decisions. For example, Dropshipping businesses, which account for 15% of Shopify businesses, mostly care about marketing data since they don't have any stock.

Pros: Would be catered to business type, potentially more useful

Cons: Businesses change all the time (a dropshipping business might not always be dropshipping)

The verdict was to section information by business vertical. This gives businesses a more accurate story of their data.

RESULTS

When the new support flow solutions rolled out, the team saw a 36% decrease in support desk tickets within 2 weeks (compared to the previous 2 week average). This support flow also provides the ability to scale with more automated flows as the team grows.
