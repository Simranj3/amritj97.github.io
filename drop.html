BACKGROUND

Drop is a rewards app that deals with user’s financial bank information in order to track transactions and apply points to their account. This makes getting rewards seamless and easy.

When relying on a 3rd party application to track those transactions, there are often issues with cards getting unlinked and points not being awarded on time. This makes it hard for users to understand how they are getting their points.


PROBLEM STATEMENT

In 2018, Drop saw an 18% increase in support desk tickets , with almost 40% of them being due to a UI misunderstanding. Knowing that Drop will be relying on this third party service for their transaction tracking for the foreseeable future, it is important for them to create a more streamlined way to help members ‘Self Serve’ when it comes to support issues.


INITIAL RESEARCH

I broke down my research into multiple parts, spanning a few days. Having already audited the app, I knew where support entrances lived and where confusion might lie.

My primary instinct was to take a look at the kinds of support tickets we were receiving. From here, I saw that this was the breakdown of support desk tickets:

I spoke to engineering to determine the cause of some of these problems. From my discussion I found that most issues were issues that could be resolved by a user (like missing points and referral points), and other issues were problems with Plaid.

I read through some of the support tickets that we were receiving, recognizing the tone and the fact that user’s simply didn’t that points took up to 8 days to appear or that to gain the referral bonus, users needed to link a card and verify their e-mail address.

There were gaps in my, and the teams understanding of the user’s confusions. I wanted to know if the user’s used the FAQ to understand their problems a little better. I sent out a survey of 8 questions, and got about 60 responses. From here, I learned that:

  • Most users do look at FAQs
  • 80% of users don't mind waiting as long as they know there's an issue

From my research, I summarized the problem as being two tiered:

Most of the support problems came from system problems (from Plaid), and from the lack of knowledge about certain things (like wait times for points). I mapped out potential solutions for each of the problem types, mostly from speaking to users about what they would find delightful.

DESIGN APPROACH

Having already done an audit of the application, I mapped out all of the screens to determine what flows cause what confusions, and how I can go about surfacing relevant information to users, in order to alleviate any confusion.

DESIGN SOLUTION 1: AUTOMATION

The first solution that I implemented was the automated flows. More than half of the support desk tickets were issues that a user could have solved. I dug into the support desk tickets and spoke to engineers to find the two biggest automation/"Self Serve" support flows.

Digging into the first one being 'Referral Points', I determined a series of 'Checks' that could take place in the backend to determine if a user needs to do something before getting referral points.

Having the automation makes the user do 4 checks before they file a support desk ticket and also allows them to figure out what they can do to get the points.



DESIGN SOLUTION 2: SYSTEM ALERTS

Going back to the initial research, almost 25% of support desk tickets were due to system failures from Plaid. Since Drop currently doesn't have engineering bandwidth to process transactions without a 3rd party, it was important to surface these system alerts within the app.



DESIGN SOLUTION 3: EDUCATION

We saw the need for education within the app, especially since 75% of users look at FAQs prior to submitting a support desk ticket. This meant that we could include the FAQs within the flow of filing a support ticket.



RESULTS

When the new support flow solutions rolled out, the team saw a 36% decrease in support desk tickets within 2 weeks (compared to the previous 2 week average). This support flow also provides the ability to scale with more automated flows as the team grows.
